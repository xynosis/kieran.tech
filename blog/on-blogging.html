# On blogging


Jan 6 2019 17:02


I've finally got around to redesigning my personal website - you would know, you're here! A big part of why I wanted to redo the website was because I was hoping to use it in a more interactive way - from the very beginning, really, I'd wanted it to be both a 'landing page' and a bit more of a blog. This post is a bit of a reflection on my experiences with blogging - from Blogspot, to Livejournal, to Wordpress - to my own system, I guess!


That's a bit disingenuous, really - mostly, this post is to get the rustiness out of my system, to write so that I have written something, to clear away the cobwebs. I'm already in terrible habits like using hyphens all over the place and typing stream of consciousness.

# Let's start at the very beginning

Way back when I was 10, I was somewhat of a giant nerd. I still am now, but back then I had a fairly successful Doctor Who news website/blog. The Dalek Genetic Labs (as it was renamed after Season 3's Daleks in Manhattan) was a fairly reputable source of news and information about new episodes of Doctor Who. It was just running on a free .co.nr domain, hosted on Blogspot, or it may have then still been Blogger. Mostly, we reposted the same news that others were posting, but with our spin or speculation.

That's right - I said we. Aged 10, I had a team of writers. God knows how or why anyone would write for/with me, especially at that age. In fairness to them, I made very sure to never mention my age - I tried to seem cool and above everything in an attempt to seem like I was older.

In large part the format of the site was modelled after Torchwood.tv, a well-known and loved source of news about Doctor Who's spin-off, Torchwood. I spoke with the guy behind Torchwood.tv before setting up my own website and tried to get tips off of him. I've no idea what he told me, but Blogger was good enough for him, so I decided it was good enough for me.

Eventually, ceased posting on the website because - and you're going to laugh about this - my mum found out I was running the site and thought that I was going to fall prey to some creepy Internet stranger, potentially one of my co-authors. A shame, really, as it really was quite a good - and surprisingly well-visited - website.

# The angsty years

In my teenage years, I hopped from social media platform to social media platform. From Bebo, to Myspace, to Piczo, to LiveJournal - it was all over the place. But LiveJournal was somewhere I could feel somewhat at home. Despite its recent drama (which I'm not sure I know much about, really), about ten years ago LiveJournal used to be the place. It was full of great people who were wildly passionate about things, who made friends and saw deeply into other people's lives through their posts.

Of course, being a teenager, most of my posts were angsting over friend drama, girls I liked who didn't like me back, or straight copypastes from MSN conversations. What on earth was I thinking?

I stayed on LiveJournal for a surprising amount of years - going from account to account (as did a large portion of LiveJournal, I think) - until eventually just stopping using it entirely. I think a part of me associated it with that far-too-guttural venting from those angsty years, and not wanting to share quite as much with the Internet anymore, I jumped ship.

# Side by side: WordPress and tumblr

I split myself in two, at that point. I didn't just stop angsting - I just did it ever so slightly more privately, indirectly, and in the form of reblogging things passive aggressively. It was basically subtweeting before subtweeting was a thing. So the angsty part of me went to tumblr, to share my heartbreak and inner monologue with a new group of strangers, and the pretentious part of me went to WordPress.

WordPress had been around before that, but it was only now that I began to pay attention to it. I suppose this is probably because I was at that age, approaching 18, where not only did I want to be pretentious, but I wanted to have a platform on which to be pretentious. Again, I set up a stupid amount of blogs, never able to fix on one. Horrifyingly, one of them still exists.

# One last thing

I remembered just a moment ago that I had one more blog - which I guess you could say is semi-active. This blog was started sometime during my undergraduate degree, and it's probably the most I've ever stuck with a single blogging platform - which, if you visit, can see isn't all that much.

Hilariously, though, I scorolled back to the first post, and it's basically this same post - talking about my experiences with blogging in the past, mentioning how I'm notoriously bad at actually committing to blogging, etc.

I'd like to think that this time will be different because it's on my own website, that I maintain, semi-regularly update, and include on all of my email signatures. Whether or not that happens still remains to be seen, I guess.

See you in a few years when I rewrite this post without remembering it exists, I guess?
